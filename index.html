Glossary of Methods and Definitions

Methodology. Indicative early-design estimates of A1–A3 (product stage / cradle-to-gate) embodied carbon only. For volume-based materials, carbon = thickness × density × factor, per m² of detail. Biogenic carbon, transport (A4), construction, in-use and end-of-life stages are not included.

Factor. A material's carbon factor is its cradle-to-gate (A1–A3) global-warming potential per unit of material — the figure entered for each layer. It is produced by a life-cycle assessment and read from an EPD or database (see Sources), not computed by this tool:

factorfactor = (A1 + A2 + A3 GWP) ÷ declared quantity

A1 = raw-material supply, A2 = transport to factory, A3 = manufacturing. The result is in kgCO₂e per kg, m², or m³ (the EPD's declared unit). You supply or override it per material.

Embodied carbon. Each layer's carbon (kgCO₂e per m² of detail) is then computed from its factor by the formula matching the material's unit:

per kglayer = thickness(m) × density(kg/m³) × factor(kgCO₂e/kg)
per m³layer = thickness(m) × factor(kgCO₂e/m³)
per m²layer = factor(kgCO₂e/m²)
detail totalper_m² = Σ layers
absolute totaltotal = per_m² × area(m²)

Thickness entered in mm or inches is converted to metres first. Worked example — 200 mm reinforced concrete: 0.200 × 2400 × 0.130 = 62.4 kgCO₂e/m².

Data. Each factor's source is shown under its layer row. Values are representative cradle-to-gate averages in the style of the ICE database (Inventory of Carbon & Energy, Circular Ecology) and are indicative only. Every factor is editable — override it with a value from a product-specific EPD (Environmental Product Declaration) before relying on results. Not a substitute for a full LCA.

Sources. The source options in “Add a material” mean:

  • EPD — Environmental Product Declaration: a standardized, independently verified datasheet of a specific product's environmental impacts (per ISO 14025 / EN 15804). The most reliable, product-specific figure. Where: in the EPD's LCA-results table, read the GWP row (kg CO₂-eq), columns A1–A3, and note the declared unit.
  • ICE — Inventory of Carbon & Energy: an open database of generic, average material factors (maintained by Circular Ecology). Useful for early design; not tied to a specific product. Where: download the free spreadsheet at circularecology.com and read the “Embodied Carbon (kgCO₂e/kg)” column. Example: clay brick ≈ 0.21 kgCO₂e/kg → in “Add a material” choose unit per kg, factor 0.21, density 1700.
  • EC3 — Embodied Carbon in Construction Calculator: a free tool (Building Transparency) that aggregates real product EPDs so you can compare actual products. Where: search a material at buildingtransparency.org and use a product's A1–A3 GWP (per its declared unit), or the category “industry average”.
  • Manufacturer data: figures published by a manufacturer (datasheet or brochure) that may not be independently third-party verified. Where: the product's datasheet, technical/specification sheet, or the maker's sustainability page — look for a stated GWP / kgCO₂e value.
  • Indicative / assumed: a representative or estimated placeholder value — verify against a real source before relying on it.
